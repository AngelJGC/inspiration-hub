# dixonandmoe/rellax

> **The smallest, battle-tested parallax engine on the web.**

- **URL:** https://github.com/dixonandmoe/rellax
- **Live:** https://dixonandmoe.com/rellax/
- **Stars:** ⭐ 7.2k+
- **Stack:** Vanilla JS (React wrappers exist)

## Techniques

- **Data-attribute driven parallax speeds** — `data-rellax-speed="-4"` on any element.
- **Per-element horizontal + vertical axis** support.
- **Z-index aware layering** for multi-plane depth.
- **Centered/percentage-based anchors** — align speed reference points precisely.

## Why inspiring

Its source is **short enough to read in one sitting** and port to React hooks. If you're
learning how "parallax at scroll = element.y * speed - scroll.y" works under the hood, this is
the 1-hour course. For production, use `react-scroll-parallax` instead.
